During my time at Berkeley (the highlights)

Ironically, most of my photo opportunities were near the end of my college career. Although, I am not sure
if anyone would want to see the running around and late night study jams photos instead.

For my graduating engineering class, I was given the opportunity to speak during the virtual ceremony. Note: This was in May 2020, so due to widespread COVID-19 lockdowns, the college opted to delay the in-person graduation ceremony but still hold an event to mark the occasion.

While helping out the College of Engineering with their semesterly Community event and undergraduate T-shirt giveaway, I and some of the Engineering Student Council Executive team members were invited to cut the ribbon to commemorate the 10th year anniversary of the opening of the Engineering Student Services. In the picture are also the Associate Dean for Equity and Inclusion, the Associate Dean for Students, and the Dean of Berkeley Engineering.

Berkeley Engineering gave me the chance to speak to the incoming class of engineers (~800 students) during the Golden Bear Orientation as the representative of the current Berkeley engineers.
