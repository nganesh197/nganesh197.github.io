Website Design/Development Projects

Imagine you are a company/non-profit/freelancer and you need a quick way to market yourself to the world. Websites are a fast, easy, and relatively cheap method to market your wares. Each website shown has a different problem at hand, from branding fixes to a full overhaul. Feel free to contact me if you need someone to construct/update/redo your website!

Northern California Center for
Occupational and Environmental Health (COEH)

For more than 40 years, the Northern Center for Occupational and Environmental Health (COEH) and its sister centers at Los Angeles and Irvine serve government, industry, schools, health professionals, and the general public through programs and partnerships designed to deepen understanding of occupational and environmental hazards and to prevent disease, fatalities, and injuries.

I was hired as a Website Developer while an undergraduate at Cal to maintain and improve the web assets of the organization. As the organization heavily used their website to advertise their programs, they greatly needed a website that could be easily updated, has a low learning curve, and is readable to wide range of viewers. Their old website was difficult to update, hard to adjust to COEH's branding, and burdensome for visitors to navigate.

  • Check out COEH's website

Key Results

  • Website was moved from Wordpress to Wix. This was due to the comfort level of the COEH staff to use Wix over Wordpress in updating the website
  • A custom template was designed for COEH to align better with its branding. Additionally, template aided in properply showcasing COEH's programs.
  • Previously lost website information was recovered using Web archives and backed up websites. This greatly helped COEH ensure their website helped its visitors


Engineering Student Council at Berkeley

Founded more than 45 years ago, Engineering Student Council represents engineering student body at Berkeley. The student organization works with Berkeley Engineering on creating an equitable and inclusive climate within the college. Additionally, ESC allocates $100,000+ funds from the ASUC, ESS, and CoE to more than 50+ organizations.

Over the two years I was on the executive team, I took the opportunity to completely revamp the website's HTML, CSS, and Javascript code. Before, the website was solely used for student organizations affiliated with the ESC to apply for funding, see how much they were allocated, and request more funding. The new website focused on providing resources to both students (undergraduate and graduate) and student organizations that universities struggle to provide.

  • Check out ESC's website

Key Results

  • Redesigned the entire site through HTML/CSS/Javascriptusing as well as add resources for students regarding academics, housing, food security, and mental health.
  • Adjusted website to be mobile friendly as well as readable for a wide range of visitors. Conducted A/B testing to verify the website's overall structure and functionality.
  • Simplified and expanded guides and passdown documents to improve efficiency in updating the website.


UC Berkeley/ UCSF Ergonomics Program

The Ergonomics Program is affiliated with the Center for Occupational and Environmental Health, a University of California umbrella organization that links faculty and research programs with research interest in occupational health from the Berkeley, Davis and San Francisco campuses. The Program's laboratory is located at U.C. Berkeley's Global Campus (Richmond Field Station), a research station administered by the College of Engineering.

As part of my work with the Center for Occupational and Environmental Health, I remade my laboratory's website. Primarily I worked on improving the overall aesthetics of the website as the original website was devoid of color. While reconstructing the website, I came up with lab's new logo (which can be seen under Design Portfolio).

  • Check out UCB/ UCSF ERGO's website

Key Results

  • Originally on Squarespace, this site was moved to Wix due to client's preference and Wix's capabilities in marketing the program.
  • Re-organized the landing page to showcase program's research, academic and consulting work as well as show the recent papers the program's research team has published.
  • Adjusted branding of the ERGO website to reflect its affiliation with UC Berkeley and UCSF by using similar fonts and colors of each those school's branding.


Also, this website was made using a template by HTML5UP.net and rewriting/adding portions in HTML/CSS/Javascript that the client (me) wanted to have on this site!
